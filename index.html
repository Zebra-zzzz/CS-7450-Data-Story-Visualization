Insights into the Gross of Movies

Start scrolling to read the data story.

This is a data story about some interesting insights into the gross of movies

Each node represents a movie. The size of each node is positively correlated with its gross. Different color represents different genres.

Adventure and action movies have the highest average gross

Adventure movies have an average gross of $125884510. Action movies have an average gross of 88840322. It is no wonder they are among the 4 most frequently shooted genres.

Comedy and drama movies are another story

Even though comedy and drama are also among the top 4 frequently shooted genres, their gross is much lower than action and adventure movies.

High gross does not mean high return of interest (ROI)

It is interesting that even though the gross of comedy and drama is not that high, but their ROI (Comedy: 1.05, Drama: 1.003) is much higher than adventure and action movies (Adventure: 0.31, Action: 0.15). And surprisingly, horror gots the highest average ROI rate of 11.46.

IMDb score does affect gross to some extent

While a higher IMDb score cannot guarantee a higer gross, but in generall a movie with a higher score is more likely to have a higher gross.

Movie duration

Interestingly, the duration of a movie does not seem to have much of an effect on gross

The top5 directors

Directors who directed Marvel, Disney, and Pixar movies got the highest gross

The top5 actors

Unlike directors, actors with the highest gross are not those who only acted in Marvel movies. Even Robert Downey who is famous for Iron Man has acted in wide variety of movies like Sherlock Holmes, Due Date.
